What is LeNet-5

   Yann LeCun, Leon Bottou, Yosuha Bengio and Patrick Haffner proposed a neural network architecture for handwritten and machine-printed character recognition in 1990â€™s which they called LeNet-5. The LeNet-5 architecture consists of two sets of convolutional and average pooling layers, followed by a flattening convolutional layer, then two fully-connected layers and finally a softmax classifier.

Neural network has the following properties:
  • 7 layers
  • 64,811 total params
  • 64,811 trainable params
  • 0 non-trainable params
  • Activation functions used: tanh, softmax
